Career

Talking of his career, Mitch was the President of Video Droid from the year 1984 to March 1998. After Video Droid, he was the VP of Business Development and Strategic Alliances for Netflix. He held this position from March 1998 to January 2003. Later he was the Senior Director and VP of Operations from May 2003 to December 2005 at McDonald’s Corporation. After McDonald’s, he worked at Redbox as the Chief Operating Officer from 2005 to 2009 and then as the President from 2009 to 2011. After his tenure was over at the Redbox, he was the CEO of Quarterly Co., and he remained there from September 2012 to July 2014.

He was also the board member of Charles Tillman Cornerstone Foundation, from September 2011 to January 2016. The organization helps needy children and families who need opportunities and resources. After he left this organization, he joined Alchemy as the board member, and he stayed there from August 2015 to April 2016. Alchemy is currently the independent distributor of the film and television in North America. Later he was from 2015 to 2017; he was on the board of Cantaloupe Systems, which is a retail payment service provider and was acquired by USAT for $80 million.

Later in June 2016, he was named the CEO of MoviePass, and then he oversaw the growth of the company’s user base customers to over 3 million subscribers. But the company’s business model struggled as it dropped to 300,000 subscribers at the near end. Lowe then instructed employees to change the user passwords in July 2018 so that the company could save money as it ran out of funds.

Then on September 13, 2019, he announced that the MoviePass would be shutting down the next day as the efforts to recapitalize the company has not been much successful. So on January 28, 2020, the MoviePass’ parent company filed for Chapter 7 Bankruptcy, and the company announced that it had ceased all the business operations, and now the company is no more.
